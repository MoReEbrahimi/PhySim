Introduction

Traffic jams that appear seemingly out of nowhere ‚Äî without an accident, construction, or any visible obstruction ‚Äî are known as phantom traffic jams or stop-and-go waves. These jams emerge from local interactions between drivers, even when all are trying to maintain steady speed.

From a physics standpoint, it's a classic example of emergent behavior: large-scale patterns arising from simple rules.

How the Simulation Works
